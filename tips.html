Safety Tips

📍 Share Live Location

Share your real-time tracking coordinates with close friends or trusted family contacts during travel via apps like WhatsApp, Google Maps, or native SOS settings.

🌙 Avoid Lonely Areas

Stick to well-lit main routes and avoid isolated shortcuts, dark alleys, or completely unpopulated public spaces during late-night travel.

📞 Save Emergency Numbers

Keep essential national helpline systems (like 112 or 1091) and personal priority emergency speed-dials readily accessible on your phone's lock screen.

🎧 Stay Aware of Your Surroundings

Avoid wearing noise-canceling headphones or staring down constantly at your phone while walking alone. Keeping your eyes and ears open helps you spot anomalies early.

🧠 Trust Your Instincts

If a person, a vehicle, or a specific environment makes you feel uneasy or uncomfortable, don't worry about being polite. Leave the situation immediately.

🔑 Keep Keys Ready & Walk Confidently

Have your house or car keys out and ready in your hand *before* you reach your door or vehicle. Walk with a confident, steady pace to signal that you are alert.
